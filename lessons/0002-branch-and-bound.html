Branch-and-Bound & the TSP TraceBranch-and-Bound 與 TSP 推演

Exam goal: hand-trace a branch-and-bound search — reduce a cost matrix to a lower bound, then walk the TSP decision tree to the optimal tour. This is the strategy that the four from Lesson 1 cannot do: it finds the best solution, not just a solution. Your tangible win: reproduce the 1-4-6-7-3-5-2-1 tour, cost 126, from a 7×7 matrix on paper.考試目標：手算推演一次 branch-and-bound 搜尋 — 把成本矩陣縮減成一個 lower bound，再沿著 TSP 決策樹走到最佳 tour。這正是第 1 課那四種策略做不到的事：它找的是最佳解，而不只是某一個解。你的具體收穫：能在紙上從一個 7×7 矩陣重現出 1-4-6-7-3-5-2-1 這條 tour，成本 126。

0 · What are we even solving?0 · 我們到底在解什麼？

Before any machinery, three plain-English ideas. Read these once and the rest of the lesson is just bookkeeping.在進入任何機制之前，先講三個白話觀念。讀懂這些之後，這一課剩下的部分就只是記帳而已。

Feasibility vs optimization. A feasibility (or decision) problem asks "is there any valid answer?" — find one solution that works, or say "none exists." An optimization problem asks for the best valid answer by some measure (cheapest, shortest, most profit). The gap matters: finding a tour is easy; finding the cheapest tour, and proving nothing cheaper exists, is the hard part.Feasibility 與 optimization。一個 feasibility（或 decision）problem 問的是「有沒有任何有效解？」 — 找出一個可行的解，或回答「不存在」。一個 optimization problem 則要求依某個標準找出最佳的有效解（最便宜、最短、利潤最大）。這個差距很重要：找出某一條 tour 很容易；找出最便宜的 tour，並證明沒有更便宜的存在，才是難的部分。

The Travelling Salesperson Problem (TSP). A salesperson must visit every city exactly once and return to the city they started from. Each leg from one city to another has a cost (think distance or price). The goal: find the cheapest such round-trip. One full round-trip — start city, through every other city once, back to start — is called a tour. TSP is an optimization problem, and a famously hard one (NP-complete).Travelling Salesperson Problem (TSP)。一位推銷員必須恰好拜訪每座城市各一次，並回到出發的城市。從一座城市到另一座的每一段都有一個 cost（可想成距離或價格）。目標：找出最便宜的這種環狀行程。一趟完整的環狀行程 — 從起點城市出發、每座其他城市各經過一次、再回到起點 — 稱為一條 tour。TSP 是一個 optimization problem，而且是出了名的難（NP-complete）。

The cost matrix. We write the costs in a grid called a cost matrix: the entry in row i, column j is the cost of going from city i to city j. The diagonal (going from a city to itself) is ∞ — you can't stay put. Here is a tiny 3-city example you can read at a glance:Cost matrix。我們把這些 cost 寫在一個格狀表裡，稱為 cost matrix：第 i 列、第 j 行的項目就是從城市 i 走到城市 j 的 cost。對角線（從一座城市走回自己）是 ∞ — 你不能原地不動。這裡有一個 3 座城市的迷你範例，一眼就能看懂：

to: 1 2 3 from 1: ∞ 7 3 (city 1 → city 2 costs 7; 1 → 3 costs 3) from 2: 4 ∞ 9 from 3: 6 2 ∞

A tour like 1 → 2 → 3 → 1 costs 7 + 9 + 6 = 22; the tour 1 → 3 → 2 → 1 costs 3 + 2 + 4 = 9. With 3 cities there are only two tours to compare, but the number of tours explodes as cities grow — so we need a smarter method than listing them all.像 1 → 2 → 3 → 1 這條 tour 的 cost 是 7 + 9 + 6 = 22；而 1 → 3 → 2 → 1 這條 tour 的 cost 是 3 + 2 + 4 = 9。只有 3 座城市時只要比較兩條 tour，但隨著城市增加，tour 的數量會爆炸性成長 — 所以我們需要比「全部列出來」更聰明的方法。

Branch-and-bound, in plain terms. Instead of listing every tour, we split the choices into a tree: each branch is a yes/no decision like "does the tour use the leg 4→6 or not?" That's the branch. For every branch we compute a lower bound — the cheapest any complete tour inside that branch could possibly cost, even in the best case. That's the bound. The payoff: if a branch's lower bound is already worse than the best complete tour we've found so far, no tour in it can win, so we throw the whole branch away unopened (we "prune" it) without exploring it. That pruning is what lets us skip most of the tree yet still prove our answer is optimal.Branch-and-bound，用白話說。我們不把每條 tour 都列出來，而是把選擇切成一棵樹：每個分支都是一個是非決策，例如「這條 tour 要不要用 4→6 這一段？」這就是 branch。對每個分支我們計算一個 lower bound — 就算在最好的情況下，這個分支裡任何一條完整 tour 最少也得花多少 cost。這就是 bound。好處在於：如果某個分支的 lower bound 已經比我們目前找到的最佳完整 tour 還糟，那它裡面任何 tour 都贏不了，於是我們連打開都不打開就把整個分支丟掉（也就是「prune（剪枝）」），完全不去探索它。正是這種 pruning 讓我們能略過大部分的樹，卻仍能證明我們的答案是最佳解。

Why a lower bound is allowed to prune. A lower bound is a guarantee: "everything in here costs at least this much." If that floor is already taller than a tour you've completed, opening the branch can only confirm bad news — so skip it. The whole skill below is just: how do we compute a trustworthy lower bound? Answer: the reduced cost matrix.為什麼 lower bound 可以拿來 prune。一個 lower bound 是一種保證：「這裡面的一切至少要花這麼多 cost。」如果這個下限已經比你完成過的某條 tour 還高，那打開這個分支只會再確認一次壞消息 — 所以直接略過。底下整套技巧其實就只是：我們要怎麼算出一個可信賴的 lower bound？答案是：reduced cost matrix。

1 · The one new idea: a bound that prunes1 · 唯一的新觀念：一個會 prune 的 bound

Branch-and-bound is best-first search (Lesson 1) plus a bound. DFS/BFS/hill-climbing/best-first all stop at the first goal they reach — fine for feasibility, useless for optimization, because the first tour found is rarely the cheapest. Branch-and-bound fixes this with two moves:Branch-and-bound 就是 best-first search（第 1 課）再加上一個 bound。DFS／BFS／hill-climbing／best-first 都在碰到第一個 goal 時就停下 — 對 feasibility 沒問題，但對 optimization 沒用，因為第一條找到的 tour 很少是最便宜的。Branch-and-bound 用兩個動作解決這件事：

BRANCH: split the solution space into subtrees (e.g. "tours WITH arc 4-6" vs "tours WITHOUT arc 4-6") BOUND: for each node compute a lower bound LB = the cheapest any tour in this subtree could possibly be if LB ≥ best complete tour found so far → PRUNE the whole subtree (it can't win)
Key exam line: for a minimization problem, each node carries a lower bound; you keep the best feasible cost found so far as an upper bound. Prune any node whose LB ≥ best-so-far — exploring it is pointless because nothing inside it can beat what you already have.考試關鍵句：對一個 minimization problem，每個 node 都帶有一個 lower bound；你把目前找到的最佳可行 cost 當成 upper bound。任何 LB ≥ best-so-far 的 node 都 prune 掉 — 探索它毫無意義，因為它裡面沒有任何東西能贏過你手上已有的。

2 · Reduced cost matrix → a lower bound2 · Reduced cost matrix → 一個 lower bound

How do we get a lower bound for "all tours"? A reduced cost matrix is what you get by subtracting a constant from each row, and then from each column, so that every row and every column contains at least one 0. Here is the intuition for why the total you subtract is a valid lower bound:我們要怎麼為「所有 tour」取得一個 lower bound？Reduced cost matrix 就是你先從每一列、再從每一行各減去一個常數，使得每一列和每一行都至少含有一個 0 後得到的矩陣。以下是為什麼你所減去的總和會是一個有效 lower bound 的直覺：

Every tour must leave each city exactly once — so it uses exactly one entry from each row. If the cheapest way out of city i costs m, then every tour pays at least m for leaving city i, no matter which tour it is. Subtracting m from row i just sets that unavoidable floor aside; it lowers every tour's cost by the same m, so it never changes which tour is cheapest. The same argument works column-by-column (every tour must enter each city exactly once). Add up everything subtracted and you have a cost every tour must pay — a lower bound. Concretely:每條 tour 都必須恰好離開每座城市一次 — 所以它在每一列恰好用到一個項目。如果離開城市 i 最便宜的方式要花 m，那麼每一條 tour 不論是哪一條，離開城市 i 至少都要付 m。從第 i 列減去 m 只是把這個無可避免的下限先擱在一旁；它把每條 tour 的 cost 都同樣降低 m，所以絕不會改變哪一條 tour 最便宜。同樣的論證逐行也成立（每條 tour 都必須恰好進入每座城市一次）。把所有減去的量加總，你就得到每一條 tour 都必須付的 cost — 一個 lower bound。具體來說：

1. From each ROW, subtract that row's minimum (now every row has a 0). 2. From each COLUMN that still has no 0, subtract that column's minimum. 3. The TOTAL subtracted is a LOWER BOUND: every tour must pay at least that much.

Reduce it exactly as in the 3-city example. First subtract each row's minimum (the cheapest way out of that city): the row minimums are 3, 4, 16, 7, 25, 3, 26, summing to 84. After that, columns 3, 4 and 7 still have no zero, so subtract their minimums 7, 1, 4, summing to 12. The total subtracted is the root lower bound:就照 3 座城市那個範例的方式縮減它。先減去每一列的最小值（離開那座城市最便宜的方式）：各列最小值是 3, 4, 16, 7, 25, 3, 26，加總為 84。之後第 3、4、7 行仍然沒有零，所以減去它們的最小值 7, 1, 4，加總為 12。減去的總和就是 root 的 lower bound：

row reductions: 3+4+16+7+25+3+26 = 84 (one min per row → every row gets a 0) col reductions: 7 + 1 + 4 = 12 (columns 3, 4, 7 still lacked a 0) ---------- ROOT LOWER BOUND = 96

Choosing the branching arc. We split on a zero-cost arc — including it adds nothing, so the "include" side keeps the bound low, while the "exclude" side is forced to pay the next-cheapest way out. Pick the zero whose exclusion hurts the most.選擇要分支的 arc。我們在一條 zero-cost arc 上分支 — 把它納入不會增加任何 cost，所以「include」這一側能讓 bound 維持得低，而「exclude」這一側則被迫付出次便宜的離開方式。挑那個被排除時傷害最大的零。

4 · A peek at 0/1-knapsack bounds (Lesson 3)4 · 偷瞄一下 0/1-knapsack 的 bound（第 3 課）

Knapsack maximizes profit, which the deck flips into a minimization of negative profit. Branch-and-bound there needs the mirror image of the TSP setup: an upper bound from any quick feasible packing (e.g. taking items 1 and 2 gives −16 — no optimum can be worse than that), and a lower bound from the fractional relaxation — allow 0 ≤ Xᵢ ≤ 1, fill greedily by profit density, and you get −18.5, which rounds to −18 since real solutions are integers. So the optimum is boxed in −18 ≤ opt ≤ −16. We trace that full tree next lesson.Knapsack 是要最大化利潤，投影片把它翻轉成最小化負利潤。那裡的 branch-and-bound 需要 TSP 設定的鏡像：一個來自任何快速可行裝法的 upper bound（例如拿物品 1 和 2 得到 −16 — 最佳解不可能比這更糟），以及一個來自 fractional relaxation 的 lower bound — 允許 0 ≤ Xᵢ ≤ 1，依利潤密度貪婪地填裝，你會得到 −18.5，由於真實解是整數所以取整為 −18。於是最佳解被框在 −18 ≤ opt ≤ −16 之間。我們下一課會推演那整棵樹。
