Channel RoutingChannel Routing（通道佈線）

A chip has pins lined up on the top and bottom of a corridor; wires connecting same-labelled pins must run along horizontal rows. Use as few rows as possible — and A* finds the minimum.晶片上有一條走道，上下兩排排著一堆 pin；要把同編號的 pin 連起來的線得走在水平的橫列上。目標是用越少橫列越好——而 A* 能找出最少的那個。

📄 Source: Alg05 · The Searching Strategies §12 (Channel Routing via A*; Fig. 6-40 / 6-46 / 6-48).

🧠 Solved by:解這題的方法： A* Algorithm

The problem問題是什麼

In VLSI chip layout, a channel is a rectangular corridor between two rows of components. Along its top edge and its bottom edge sit terminals (pins). Each terminal carries a label, a small integer — pins that share a label belong to the same net and must all be electrically connected by wire.在 VLSI 晶片佈局裡，channel 是兩排元件中間一條長方形的走道。它的上緣和下緣排著一些 terminal（也就是 pin）。每個 terminal 都帶一個編號（一個小整數）——編號一樣的 pin 屬於同一個 net，必須用線把它們全部接通。

  • A net = the group of all terminals (top and bottom) bearing the same label. Every net must be wired up.net＝所有（上面和下面）同編號 terminal 的集合。每個 net 都得接起來。
  • A track = one horizontal row inside the channel along which a wire can run. A net's wire runs mostly along a single track, with short vertical "drops" up to its top/bottom pins.track＝channel 裡一條可以走線的水平橫列。一個 net 的線大部分就沿著一條 track 走，再用短短的垂直「下接」連到它上下的 pin。
  • Two nets may share a track only if their horizontal spans don't overlap — otherwise their wires would short together. That is an illegal wiring.兩個 net 要共用一條 track，前提是它們的水平範圍不重疊——不然兩條線會短路在一起，這種佈線是不合法的。

Goal: assign every net to a track so that no two overlapping nets land on the same track, and minimize the total number of tracks. Fewer tracks means a shorter channel and a smaller chip. This problem is NP-complete.目標：把每個 net 分配到某條 track 上，讓任何兩個會重疊的 net 都不落在同一條 track，並且讓 track 的總數最少。track 越少，channel 越短、晶片越小。這題是 NP-complete。

The concrete instance (Fig. 6-40). A channel with 13 columns. Each column lists its top pin and bottom pin (0 = no pin):具體的例子（Fig. 6-40）。一條有 13 行（column）的 channel。每一行列出它上面的 pin 和下面的 pin（0 = 沒有 pin）：

Reading every net's two pins gives these 8 horizontal spans (columns the wire must cover): net 1 = cols 11–13, net 2 = cols 10–11, net 3 = cols 6–9, net 4 = cols 1–6, net 5 = cols 5–12, net 6 = cols 7–12, net 7 = cols 3–4, net 8 = cols 2–8. (E.g. net 1's pins are at col 11 top and col 13 bottom, so its wire stretches across cols 11–13.) There are 8 nets, so a naïve routing that gives each net its own track needs 8 tracks; the optimal routing packs everything into just 4 tracks — and we'll see below why 4 is both achievable and impossible to beat.把每個 net 的兩個 pin 讀出來，就得到這 8 條水平範圍（線必須蓋住的 column）：net 1＝cols 11–13、net 2＝cols 10–11、net 3＝cols 6–9、net 4＝cols 1–6、net 5＝cols 5–12、net 6＝cols 7–12、net 7＝cols 3–4、net 8＝cols 2–8。（例如 net 1 的 pin 在第 11 行上面、第 13 行下面，所以它的線橫跨 cols 11–13。）總共有 8 個 net，所以最笨的做法是每個 net 各占一條 track，要 8 條 track；但最佳佈線只要 4 條 track 就全塞得下——下面會看到為什麼 4 既做得到、又不可能再更少。

Local density — the key quantity. Pick any column and count how many nets' spans pass through it: that count is the local density at that column. Since every net covering a column needs its own track right there (their wires can't overlap), the maximal local density — the largest count over all 13 columns — is a hard lower bound on the number of tracks. For this instance:Local density——關鍵的量。隨便挑一個 column，數一數有幾個 net 的範圍穿過它，這個數字就是那一行的 local density。因為蓋住同一行的每個 net 在那裡都得各占一條 track（線不能重疊），所以 maximal local density（13 行裡最大的那個數）就是 track 數的硬下界。以這個例子來說：

  • Col 6 is covered by nets {3,4,5,8} → density 4. Cols 7 and 8 are also {3,5,6,8} → density 4, and col 11 is {1,2,5,6} → density 4.第 6 行被 {3,4,5,8} 蓋住 → density 4。第 7、8 行是 {3,5,6,8} → density 4，第 11 行是 {1,2,5,6} → density 4。
  • No column reaches 5, so the maximal local density is 4. That alone proves you can never route this channel in fewer than 4 tracks — which is exactly why the optimal answer turns out to be 4.沒有任何一行到 5，所以 maximal local density 是 4。光是這點就證明了 這條 channel 絕對不可能用少於 4 條 track 解決——這也正是為什麼最佳解剛好是 4。

Two helper graphs capture the constraints.用兩張輔助 graph 把限制條件抓出來。

  • The Horizontal Constraint Graph (HCG): a node per net; a directed edge u → v means u's span lies entirely to the left of v's (they don't overlap, so they could share a track with u on the left, v on the right). So an HCG edge marks two nets that are compatible — packable together — not in conflict. A set of nets that are all pairwise ordered this way is a clique; because every pair is non-overlapping, the whole clique fits onto one single track, threaded left-to-right. The maximal cliques here are {7,3,1}, {7,5}, and {8,1} — e.g. {7,3,1} works because net 7 (cols 3–4) ends before net 3 (cols 6–9) starts, which ends before net 1 (cols 11–13) starts. Important: a clique is about fitting nets onto one track; it is a different idea from local density (which counts overlapping nets at a column to lower-bound the track count). Don't confuse the two.Horizontal Constraint Graph（HCG，水平限制圖）：每個 net 一個節點；一條有向邊 u → v 的意思是 u 的範圍整段在 v 的左邊（兩者不重疊，所以可以共用一條 track，u 在左、v 在右）。所以一條 HCG 邊標示的是兩個相容、可以塞在一起的 net，不是衝突。一組 net 如果兩兩都這樣排得出先後，就叫一個 clique；因為每一對都不重疊，整個 clique 可以塞進同一條 track，由左到右串起來。這裡的 maximal clique 是 {7,3,1}、{7,5}、{8,1}——例如 {7,3,1} 成立，是因為 net 7（cols 3–4）結束後 net 3（cols 6–9）才開始，net 3 結束後 net 1（cols 11–13）才開始。注意：clique 講的是把 net 塞進同一條 track，跟 local density 是兩回事（density 數的是同一行重疊的 net，用來給 track 數一個下界）。別把兩者搞混。
  • The Vertical Constraint Graph (VCG): an edge top-net → bottom-net wherever a column has one net on top and another on the bottom, forcing the top net into a track above the bottom net. Here the VCG is just 1→2, 3→4, 5→6 (e.g. "net 3's track must sit above net 4's"); nets 7 and 8 are unconstrained vertically. Note: the VCG decides the vertical order of tracks (who's above whom). In this small instance it never blocks a track from forming, so the A* trace below doesn't lean on it — treat it as background here; it becomes the binding constraint in denser channels (and is why a channel can sometimes be impossible without "doglegs"). How would it ever block a track? Picture two columns forcing a→b in one and b→a in another: then a must sit above b and below it — a cycle in the VCG that no ordering of whole-net tracks can satisfy, so you'd have to split a net across two tracks (a "dogleg") to break the loop. Our instance's VCG is just three independent edges with no cycle, so this never bites here.Vertical Constraint Graph（VCG，垂直限制圖）：只要某一行上面是一個 net、下面是另一個 net，就拉一條邊 上面的net → 下面的net，逼上面那個 net 走的 track 要在下面那個 net 上方。這裡的 VCG 就只有 1→2、3→4、5→6（例如「net 3 的 track 要在 net 4 上面」）；net 7 和 8 在垂直方向沒有限制。提醒：VCG 決定的是 track 之間的上下順序（誰在誰上面）。在這個小例子裡，它從來沒有擋掉任何一條 track 的組成，所以下面的 A* 過程其實沒用到它——這裡把它當背景知識就好；在更密的 channel 裡它才會變成真正卡住你的限制（也是為什麼有些 channel 不開「dogleg」就解不出來）。它到底怎麼擋掉一條 track？想像有兩行分別逼出 a→b 和 b→a：那 a 就得同時在 b 上面又在它下面——VCG 裡出現一個環，任何「整條 net 各占一條 track」的排法都滿足不了，你只好把某個 net 拆到兩條 track 上（開「dogleg」）來打破這個迴圈。我們這個例子的 VCG 只是三條互不相干、沒有環的邊，所以這裡完全不會卡。

Watch it solved看它怎麼解

First the HCG of this channel (nodes = nets, arrows = "fits left of"); the three maximal cliques {7,3,1}, {7,5}, {8,1} are the three candidate first tracks (each is a full track-load of compatible nets). Then A* searches a tree where each node commits one more track, and the branching rule is: a node's children are the maximal sets of still-unrouted nets that legally fit on the next track (pairwise non-overlapping). "Maximal" means you can't add another leftover net without an overlap — not "largest". Several different maximal sets can therefore exist side by side as siblings, and they need not be the same size: under B's leftovers {2,4,5,6,8}, both {8,2} and the lone {5} are maximal (net 5 spans cols 5–12 and clashes with every other leftover, so it can't pick up a partner — yet it still counts as a full, valid track choice). That's why a singleton like {5} sits as a sibling of a two-net set like {8,2}. Each node is scored f = g + h:先看這條 channel 的 HCG（節點 = net，箭頭 = 「排在左邊」）；那三個 maximal clique {7,3,1}、{7,5}、{8,1} 就是三個候選的第一條 track（每個都是一整條 track 塞滿相容的 net）。接著 A* 搜尋一棵樹，每往下一層就決定多一條 track，分支規則是：一個節點的小孩，就是把還沒佈的 net 塞進下一條 track 的各種最大合法組合（兩兩不重疊）。這裡的「maximal（最大）」是指『再也塞不進任何一個剩下的 net 而不重疊』，不是『net 數最多』。所以好幾組不同的 maximal 集合可以同時當兄弟節點並排，而且大小不必一樣：在 B 剩下的 {2,4,5,6,8} 底下，{8,2} 和孤零零的 {5} 都是 maximal（net 5 橫跨 cols 5–12，跟其他每個剩下的 net 都打架，找不到夥伴——但它照樣是一個完整、合法的 track 選擇）。這就是為什麼像 {5} 這種單一 net 會跟 {8,2} 這種兩個 net 的集合並列當兄弟。每個節點用 f = g + h 評分：

  • g = the tree level = how many tracks this node has committed so far.g = 樹的層數 = 這個節點到目前為止已經決定了幾條 track。
  • h = the maximal local density of the nets not yet routed. Once a net is placed on a track, delete its span and recount the densest remaining column — that count is how many more tracks are still unavoidable. Because it's a real lower bound, h never overestimates → A* returns the optimum.h = 還沒佈的那些 net 的 maximal local density。一個 net 被放上某條 track 後，就把它的範圍刪掉，重新數剩下最密的 column——那個數字就是還躲不掉的 track 數。因為它是真正的下界，h 絕不高估 → A* 保證回傳最佳解。

Worked path B → E → H → I (the winning branch). B: track 1 = {7,3,1}, so g=1; remaining nets {2,4,5,6,8} are densest at col 6 ({4,5,8}) → h=3, f=4. E: track 2 = {8,2}, g=2; remaining {4,5,6} densest at col 7 ({5,6}) → h=2, f=4. (Sibling D = {4,2} leaves {5,6,8} which still pile 3-deep at col 7 → h=3, f=5 — worse, so A* prefers E.) H: track 3 = {5}, g=3; remaining {4,6} — net 4 (cols 1–6) and net 6 (cols 7–12) are disjoint, so any single column holds at most one of them → h=1, f=4. I: track 4 = {4,6}, g=4; nothing remains → h=0, f=4. I is a goal with f=4, and no other open node has smaller f, so A* stops: 4 tracks, optimal.走一遍 B → E → H → I（最後勝出的那條路）。B：track 1 = {7,3,1}，所以 g=1；剩下的 net {2,4,5,6,8} 在第 6 行最密（{4,5,8}）→ h=3、f=4。E：track 2 = {8,2}，g=2；剩 {4,5,6} 在第 7 行最密（{5,6}）→ h=2、f=4。（隔壁的 D = {4,2} 留下 {5,6,8}，在第 7 行還是疊 3 層 → h=3、f=5——比較差，所以 A* 偏好 E。）H：track 3 = {5}，g=3；剩 {4,6}，net 4（cols 1–6）和 net 6（cols 7–12）不重疊 → h=1、f=4。I：track 4 = {4,6}，g=4；沒東西剩了 → h=0、f=4。I 是目標、f=4，而且沒有別的待展開節點 f 更小，所以 A* 停下：4 條 track，最佳解。

Why B and not A or C? All three root children A {8,1}, B {7,3,1}, C {7,5} tie at f=4, so any of them is a valid first expansion — A* only promises it will never expand a node whose f exceeds the optimum (4), and it never does. We follow B here just because it reaches a goal at f=4; expanding A or C first would also have found a 4-track solution (their subtrees can only produce f≥4 leaves too, since 4 is the density lower bound), just via a different route. Ties like this mean the order among equal-f nodes is a free choice; the cost of the answer is the same. That's exactly why A* never needs to fully expand A's or C's subtrees: once a goal pops off the frontier at the frontier's smallest f, no still-open f=4 node could ever do better.為什麼是走 B，不是 A 或 C？root 的三個小孩 A {8,1}、B {7,3,1}、C {7,5} 全都打平在 f=4，所以先展開哪一個都合法——A* 只保證它絕不會去展開 f 比最佳解（4）還大的節點，而它確實沒有。這裡選 B 只是因為它在 f=4 就摸到目標；先展開 A 或 C 其實也找得到 4 條 track 的解（它們的子樹一樣只會長出 f≥4 的葉子，因為 4 就是 density 下界），只是換一條路而已。像這種平手，代表同 f 節點之間誰先誰後是隨你挑的；答案的成本都一樣。這也正是為什麼 A* 根本不必把 A 或 C 的子樹整個展開完：只要有個目標以當下 frontier 最小的 f 被取出來，任何還沒展開的 f=4 節點都不可能更好。
