Travelling Salesperson — Branch & Bound

Given a table of city-to-city travel costs, find the cheapest round trip that visits every city exactly once and returns home — proven optimal without trying all the orderings.

📄 Source: Alg05 · Ch 6 §9 (TSP via Branch-and-Bound), Tables 6-3/6-4/6-5, Fig. 6-25/6-26.

🧠 Solved by: Branch-and-Bound

The problem

A salesperson must visit n cities and come back to where they started. We're given an n×n cost matrix where entry (i, j) is the cost of travelling from city i directly to city j (the diagonal is ∞ — you can't go from a city to itself). A tour is an order in which you visit all the cities, ending back at the start; its cost is the sum of the arc costs along the way.

The task: find the tour of minimum total cost. This is the Travelling Salesperson Problem (TSP), and it is NP-complete — there are (n−1)! possible tours, so brute force is hopeless even for modest n.

The concrete instance (Table 6-3). Seven cities, with this cost matrix (rows = "from", columns = "to", ∞ on the diagonal):

to: 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 from 1: ∞ 3 93 13 33 9 57 from 2: 4 ∞ 77 42 21 16 34 from 3: 45 17 ∞ 36 16 28 25 from 4: 39 90 80 ∞ 56 7 91 from 5: 28 46 88 33 ∞ 25 57 from 6: 3 88 18 46 92 ∞ 7 from 7: 44 26 33 27 84 39 ∞

We want the cheapest tour through all 7 cities. The answer turns out to be the tour 1→4→6→7→3→5→2→1 at cost 126 — but the interesting part is how Branch-and-Bound proves no cheaper tour exists without enumerating all 6! = 720 orderings.

The key tool — a lower bound from the reduced cost matrix. Every tour must leave each city once, so it pays at least the cheapest exit from each city. Subtract each row's minimum from that row (now every row has a zero), then subtract each column's minimum from any column still without a zero. The total amount subtracted is cost that any tour is forced to pay — a valid lower bound. For this instance the row reductions sum to 84 and the column reductions add 7+1+4 = 12, for a root lower bound of 96.

Watch it solved

The 7×7 cost matrix reduces to a lower bound of 96. Then we branch on include/exclude decisions for arcs, carrying a lower bound at each node. Dimmed = pruned (its bound can't beat the best tour found). The left spine of "include" choices reaches the optimal tour. Step through.

Cities: 7 Root lower bound: 96 Optimal tour: 1-4-6-7-3-5-2-1 Optimal cost: 126 Hardness: NP-complete
