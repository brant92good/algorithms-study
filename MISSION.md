# Mission

**Pass the Algorithms final exam (~2026-06-20, one week out) with confident command of the four exam topics.**

## Who & why
- User studying for a university Algorithms final (R.C.T. Lee, *Introduction to the Design and Analysis of Algorithms* — Ch 6, 7, 8 + Prune-and-Search).
- Goal is exam performance, not long-term mastery — but we still use retrieval/spacing because cramming-by-rereading fails on this material.

## Exam shape (drives every lesson)
1. **Hand-trace algorithms** (primary) — run branch-and-bound on a matrix, fill a DP table, draw the search/semantic tree, simulate selection.
2. **Minimal proofs** — mostly reductions in NP-completeness (show A → B).
3. **Short QA + quick explanation** — definitions, principle of optimality, P/NP/NP-hard/NPC, prune-and-search idea.

## Topic sequence (follow course materials)
1. **Ch6 — Searching Strategies** (Alg05): backtracking trees (SAT/Hamiltonian/subset), DFS/BFS, hill-climbing, best-first, **branch-and-bound** (personnel, TSP, 0/1 knapsack), **A\***.
2. **Prune-and-Search** (Alg06): general method + complexity, **selection / median-of-medians (O(n))**, 2-variable linear programming.
3. **Ch7 — Dynamic Programming** (Alg07): multistage graph (forward/backward), **principle of optimality**, 0/1 knapsack DP.
4. **Ch8 — NP-Completeness** (Alg08): P/NP/NP-hard/NPC, reduction, nondeterministic (guess+check) algorithms, Cook's theorem, **the reduction chain**.

## Teaching stance
- Lessons skew to **doing** (trace it yourself) with a short retrieval-QA at the end.
- Interleave older topics into later sessions — don't silo "learn all" then "test all".
